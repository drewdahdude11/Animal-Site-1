Crocodiles!

Crocodiles are large, carnivorous reptiles that live in tropical regions of Africa, Asia, the Americas, and Australia. They are known for their powerful jaws, armored bodies, and stealthy hunting skills. Crocodiles spend much of their time in freshwater habitats like rivers, lakes, and swamps, where they lie in wait for prey such as fish, birds, and mammals. As cold-blooded animals, they rely on external heat sources to regulate their body temperature. Crocodiles have existed for millions of years and are considered living fossils due to their ancient lineage.

Where Can You Find Crocodiles?

  • Rivers
  • Lakes
  • Swamps
  • Marshes

Fun Facts

  1. Crocodiles can live over 70 years
  2. Crocodiles have existed for over 200 million years, surviving mass extinctions.
  3. Crocodiles swallow chunks of food whole, and sometimes swallow stones to help digest it.
  4. Crocodiles have one of the strongest bite forces of any animal! Over 3,000 psi.
